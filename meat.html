The Meat Industry

From Industrial Farm to Table

Let's look at how your steak made it to your plate

Gone are the days of cows grazing on open pasteurs. Instead, they are raised on large industrial farms. A baby calf, after being ripped from its mother is branded, dehorned, and castrated before being placed on a large feedlot.

The cattle are fed an unnatural diet to reach the market weight of 1,200 pounds in just six months. Once they reach the weight, they are shipped off to slaughter houses.

It is here, where the beautiful cow becomes a lifeless piece of meat that is shipped off to your local supermarket where the consumer purchases the cleanly plastic wrapped flesh to eat.
